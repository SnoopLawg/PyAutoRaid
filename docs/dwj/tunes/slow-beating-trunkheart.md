# Slow Beating Trunkheart

**Type**: Unkillable · **Difficulty**: Easy · **Key**: 2 Key UNM · **Affinity**: All Affinities · **Author**: DeadwoodJedi

Source: https://deadwoodjedi.com/speed-tunes/slow-beating-trunkheart/

With the same speeds as the original Tower/Skullcrusher Unkillable and Slow Hellcat Unkillable, Emic Trunkheart creates an Unkillable team by utilizing his cooldown ability to enable another AOE Unkillable or Block Damage champ to work at insanely low speeds.

## Slot Rules

### Slot 1 · Emic Trunkheart

Needs a3 booked to 4 turn cooldowns.

### Slot 2 · Doompriest

The Cleanser will always go first after AOE2 to cleanse debuffs and/or to cleanse the Stun. Doompriest can cleanse both and as such is an ideal fit to make this affinity friendly.


If you’re using a Debuff Blocker instead of a Cleanser, you can use an additional DPS in this spot instead.

### Slot 3 · DPS

Alternate Unkillable Champion can go here.

### Slot 4 · Unkillable

Alternate Unkillable Champion can go here.

### Slot 5 · Debuff Blocker

If you’re using a Cleanser instead of a Debuff Blocker, you can use this slot for additional DPS.


If using Warcaster or other 1 turn Block Damage/Unkillable champ they MUST go here.

## Notes

Works for both UNM and NM. Emic Trunkheart will move at a 4:3 Speed Ratio, enabling him to keep unkillable on your full team for the AOE attacks while cooling down your allies abilities, allowing the usage of another AOE unkillable champion to stay protected for the stun.

These speeds and descriptions listed encompass a wide range of viable options, but not all of them.  Spirit can be particularly tricky depending on whether you use a Cleanser or 1 Turn Duration Block Debuff or a 2 Turn Duration Block Debuff.  Triple check!

Speed Tune Sync:



UNM tune will sync on turn 5
NM tune will sync on turn 2

Spirit affinity on NM, when using a Cleanser, will alter the order and timing of the unkillable skills, but it is still viable.

If you’re using Block Debuff Champion on a 3 turn cooldown (Underpriest Brogni/Grizz Jarl/Tatura Rimehide) instead of a cleanser, beware the timing of Emic’s A2 cooldown as that can throw off the timing of the Block Debuff skill – you will likely have to manual the setup between turns 3 and turn 5.

If you’re using a Block Debuff Champion on a 4 turn cooldown timing shouldn’t be an issue.

A 1 turn Block Debuff will work, but Emic will protect the Stun and AOE 1, not both AOE’s.  Must have another aoe UK/BD champ.

Regardless of the cooldowns of the champions, be careful of buffs that affect stun targeting (Increase Defense, Reflect Damage) as the clan boss will try to avoid these.  The first few turns Emic will be vulnerable to the stun since these buffs will likely fall off him while staying on others (making him a preferred stun target).  Either manual with him or your Debuff Blocker or account for the skill delay from the stun in your setup phase.


Check Advanced Notes for special conditions for solo unkillable champions (like Skullcrusher or Taurus)


Skill Set Up:



UNM

Emic opens a3, then a2.
Other Unkillable Champ has a 1 turn delay with UK/BD skill (open with a1 or other skill).
If using Demytha, prioritize the a2 skill and open a2, then a3.
If using a 3 turn cooldown Block Debuff champ, open with this skill (Emic will be vulnerable to the stun).  Emic will be vulnerable to a spirit debuff on turn 5, but it will still sync up by turn 8.
If using a 4 turn cooldown Block Debuff champ, open with this skill (Emic will be vulnerable to the stun).


NM

Emic opens a1, a3, then a2.
Other Unkillable Champ has a 2 turn delay with UK/BD skill (open with a1 or other skill, twice).
If using Demytha, prioritize the a2 skill and open a1, then a2, then a3.
If using a 3 turn cooldown Block Debuff champ, has a 1 turn delay of this skill (open with a1 or other skill), will still sync up by turn 2.
If using a 4 turn cooldown Block Debuff champ, has a 1 turn delay of this skill (open with a1 or other skill), will still sync up by turn 2.


NM Spirit with Cleanser ONLY

Emic opens a3, then a2.
Other Unkillable Champ has a 1 turn delay with UK/BD skill (open with a1 or other skill).
If using Demytha, prioritize the a2 skill and open a1, then a2, then a3.
Turn order will sync on turn 8.

Advanced Notes:



For those of you who want to get creative and try using a solo unkillable champ to tank the stun – think Skullcrusher or Gnarlhorn – you will want to know there’s some alternate speeds that will help you against spirit affinity on NM.  Your fast DPS (175-178) actually has two distinct ranges:  175-178 and 179-181.  If you’re using a Cleanser, the traditional ranges will not work for you, as the Unkillable from Emic will shift, covering the Stun and first AOE by turn 8.  When using a second AOE unkillable champion (2 turn duration) this isn’t an issue – they still cover everyone.  But if you want to solo tank the stun you will need the fast DPS champ between 179-181 for Nightmare on Spirit affinity to stay perfectly in sync.  However, over 179 on UNM will delay syncing by one full cycle on void, so you’ll have to pick your poison, so to speak. When blocking the Spirit affinity debuff, you’re best staying 178 and under.  The Nightmare link will be shared below.
When using a solo unkillable champion (like Skullcrusher or Taurus) in addition to the notes above, you will have to ensure the champion heals enough to be attacked, but not enough where the clan boss will select an alternate target. The Clan Boss will avoid champs with Unkillable Buffs if their HP is under 16% of Max.

Some champions with passive skills (like Skullcrown) will not need any special gear – they simply CANNOT HEAL. So avoid using leech or any healing masteries or healing skills.
Champions who place unkillable on themselves (like Skullcrusher) will need to have enough passive healing to exceed the 16% threshold for the Clan Boss to consider them a viable target.  Using a Regeneration and Immortal set combination should suffice.  If your cleanser heals (like Doompriest) you can factor that in as well.  But you should avoid the life drinker mastery as it can potentially over-heal.  In addition the rest of your team needs to heal up more than your target.  The easiest method to achieve this is through the classic Lifesteal set or, if the unkillable skill is NOT an attack skill, leech debuff. Note that if leech falls off chances are you will wipe!
Special notice should be made for a champion that harms themselves like Taurus.  You will have to use his Unkillable skill a turn earlier, so the next turn he has a chance to heal up above the threshold.  Leech should be avoided when using him.
Also note champions like Dhukk, who place buffs on themselves the clan boss tries to avoid (reflect damage in his case) will NOT work since the CB will avoid stunning them regardless.
Lastly AFFINITY MATTERS! Don’t choose someone who’s strong affinity – they will NOT take the stun!


NM Spirit the turn order will differ from other affinities if using a cleanser – it WILL change skill order so make sure to pre-set those changes.
